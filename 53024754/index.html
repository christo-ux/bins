Farata recipe

Farata is the Mauritian version of Indian paratha, a layered, flaky, pan-fried flatbread. It consists of three basic ingredients: flour, water, and oil. Faratas are traditionally served warm, accompanying spicy curry dishes or any kind of chutney. The farata itself taste so good, so when you add spicy curry such as fish or chicken curry, accompanied with turban squash which melts in the mouth, its like an explosion of flavour with a mixture of texture.

List of ingrediens needed.

  • 1kg of white flour.
  • 800ml of warm water.
  • 4 tablespoon of butter (melted).
  • salt and vegetable oil to adjust.
